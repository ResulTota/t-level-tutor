Subtopic 5.2: Digital value to organisations

5.2.1 - Understand how digital systems are used to support key organisation areas

Sales and Marketing:

    Better market research

    Digital tools such as online surveys, customer feedback systems, and analytics allow organisations to understand customer needs and identify market trends.

    Better brand promotion, including social media

    Platforms such as Instagram, TikTok, and LinkedIn allow businesses to promote products and engage with wider audiences more cost-effectively.

    Online selling

    E-commerce websites and marketplaces (e.g., Amazon, Shopify) provide organisations with the ability to sell goods and services 24/7 worldwide.

    Contextualising customer behaviour to personalise services offered

    By using customer data, organisations can provide personalised recommendations, targeted advertisements, and customised offers to increase satisfaction and sales.

    Better customer retention

    Through loyalty programs, CRM systems, and personalised communication, businesses can build long-term relationships with their customers.

    Brand differentiation and values

    Digital platforms allow companies to emphasise unique values such as innovation, sustainability, or customer service to stand out from competitors.

    Use of analytic tools, including search and social media analytics

    Analytics tools like Google Analytics and social media insights help organisations track engagement, customer behaviour, and marketing success.

Research, Design and Development:

    Digital prototyping, simulations, and customer feedback tools allow the creation of unique, innovative products and services tailored to market needs.

Human Resources:

    Staff records

    HR systems store employee data securely for easy access and compliance with laws.

    Performance management

    Digital performance tools help track employee achievements, goals, and reviews.

    Training records

    Organisations use e-learning platforms and HR databases to manage training progress and certifications.

Operations:

    Enhanced internal communication

    Messaging and video platforms (e.g., Slack, Teams) improve communication across teams.

    Automation of internal processes

    Digital systems automate routine tasks such as payroll, scheduling, and reporting.

    Automated manufacturing

    Robotics and computer-controlled machinery increase productivity and reduce errors.

    Remote working

    Cloud systems and video conferencing tools allow flexible working from any location.

    Intranet/shared workspace

    Provides staff with centralised access to company documents, policies, and news.

    Document sharing and online shared storage

    Cloud services like Google Drive and SharePoint enable real-time collaboration on documents.

Management:

Managers benefit from real-time monitoring of key performance indicators (KPIs) including:

  • Sales – live dashboards showing current revenue performance.
  • Customers served – instant tracking of service and support activities.
  • Units manufactured – automated counters showing production output.
  • Real-time location of assets – GPS and IoT systems for tracking equipment or vehicles.

Logistics:

Automated stock control systems track inventory levels, send reorder alerts, and reduce errors caused by manual entry.

Finance:

    Reduced costs

    Automation and digital workflows reduce labour costs and operational inefficiencies.

    Increased revenue

    Digital sales platforms and better customer targeting help expand revenue streams.

    Better financial reporting via up-to-date information

    Real-time accounting software ensures accurate financial insights, aiding faster decision-making.

5.2.2 - Understand how digital systems are used to meet user needs and ensure quality of product/service

Appropriate and Effective Functionality

    Digital systems must have the right functions so users can complete all required tasks.

    Example:

    Microsoft Word allows users to type, edit, format, and share documents. If the system lacked these, users would struggle to meet their needs.

Reduction of Pain Points

    Response Time

    Systems should respond quickly, or at least communicate expected wait times.

    Example: Google Drive shows a progress bar when uploading files.

    Complexity of Task

    Tasks should be as simple as possible, avoiding unnecessary steps.

    Example: Amazon provides one-click purchasing to reduce complexity.

Appropriate Accessibility Provision

    Systems must be accessible to all users, including those with disabilities.

    Example: Screen readers, captions, and high-contrast options allow broader use.

Compatibility

    With Internal Legacy Systems

    New systems should work with older systems still in use.

    Example: A payroll system pulling data from an old HR database.

    With Proposed Future Systems

    Systems should integrate with upcoming technologies.

    Example: A CRM designed to later connect with AI analytics tools.

    With External Services

    Must integrate with third-party services.

    Example: An e-commerce platform linking with PayPal and shipping providers.

Availability of Service

    Minimise Downtime

    Reliable systems should stay available as much as possible.

    Example: AWS uses backup servers to reduce outages.

    Future Proofing for Upgrades

    Systems should allow for updates without disrupting users.

    Example: Software that installs updates overnight to avoid downtime.

Effective End User Support

    Users should have help through guides, FAQs, or chat support.

    Example: Apple offers online chat, tutorials, and forums for support.

Ease of Installation

    Systems should be simple to install with minimal effort.

    Example: Mobile apps install in one click; larger software includes setup packages with documentation.

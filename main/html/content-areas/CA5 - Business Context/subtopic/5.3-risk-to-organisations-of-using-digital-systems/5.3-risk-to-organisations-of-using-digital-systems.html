Subtopic 5.3: Risk to Organisations of Using Digital Systems

5.3.1 Understand the potential risks to organisations when using digital systems

Security breaches

  • Compromised confidentiality: Sensitive information, such as customer data or trade secrets, can be exposed. Example: A hack on a healthcare provider could reveal patient records.
  • Loss of integrity: Data can be altered or corrupted, either accidentally or maliciously. Example: A financial database could be tampered with, leading to incorrect records.
  • Reduced availability: Systems might become unavailable due to attacks like Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS), preventing access for employees or customers.

Privacy breaches

    Personal information: Breaches can expose customer or employee personal data, such as addresses, phone numbers, or financial information.

    Business information: Sensitive organisational information, like intellectual property or strategic plans, can be leaked, harming competitive advantage.

Regulatory and legal non-compliance

Failing to comply with laws and regulations can result in fines, legal action, or loss of licences. Example: Not following GDPR regulations when handling personal data.

Audience exclusion

    Biases: Algorithms or AI tools can unintentionally discriminate, leading to unfair treatment of certain groups.

    Poor user experience: Systems that are difficult to navigate or inaccessible can discourage users, limiting engagement or sales.

Emerging rival technologies

Failure to adopt new technologies can result in competitors gaining an advantage and loss of market share. Example: Businesses ignoring AI automation may be outperformed by competitors who use it efficiently.

Technical issues

    Reliance and system failure: Over-reliance on a single system can be risky if it fails. Example: If a cloud service goes down, employees may be unable to work.

    System not fit for purpose: Implementing a system that does not meet organisational needs can reduce productivity and create inefficiencies.

5.3.2 Understand the potential impact of risks to organisations when using digital systems

  • Legal action: Organisations may face lawsuits from customers, employees, or regulators if they fail to protect data or comply with laws.
  • Fines: Regulatory bodies can impose financial penalties for breaches of laws like GDPR or industry-specific regulations.
  • Reputational damage: News of a breach or failure can harm public perception, leading to loss of customer trust.
  • Withdrawal of licence to practise: Certain industries, such as healthcare or finance, may lose their legal right to operate if they fail to meet compliance standards.
  • Loss of business: Customers may switch to competitors, contracts may be lost, and revenue may decline.

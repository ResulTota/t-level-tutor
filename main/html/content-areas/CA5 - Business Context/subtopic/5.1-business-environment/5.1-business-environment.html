Subtopic 5.1: Business Environment

5.1.1 - Know the purpose and sectors of different types of organisations

Purpose of the organisation:

Providing a Service

    Definition:

    An organisation offers non-physical, intangible services to meet customer needs.

    Examples:

    NHS (healthcare), Barclays (banking), Uber (transport).

Providing a Product

    Definition:

    An organisation produces or sells physical goods to customers.

    Examples:

    Tesco (groceries), Nike (sportswear), Toyota (cars).

Type of organisations in:

Private sectors

    Small or Medium-Sized Enterprises (SME)

      Independently owned businesses with fewer employees and smaller turnover.

    Large Enterprises

      Large-scale companies, often multinational, with significant resources and influence.

    Non-Governmental Organisations (NGO)

      Independent from government, typically focused on social, environmental, or humanitarian issues.

Public sectors

    Government-owned and funded, providing services to the public.

Voluntary/Charity sectors

    Organisations where income is reinvested into their cause rather than distributed as profit.

5.1.2 - Know the names and definitions of different business models

Business to Customer (B2C)

Definition:

Businesses sell products or services directly to individual consumers.

Examples:

eBay, local retail stores.

Key Features

  • Focus on individual customer needs and peferences.
  • Involves mass marketing and advertising to attract customers.
  • Usually deals with smaller, individual transactions.

Business to Business (B2B)

Definition:

A business model where a company sells products or services to other businesses.

Examples:

Wholesale suppliers, Microsoft, IBM, Saleforce.

Key Features

  • Focus on building long-term relationships with other businesses.
  • Involves larger transactions and contracts.
  • Marketing strategies are often more targeted and relationship-based.

Business to Many (B2M)

Definition:

A business model where a company sells to both individual consumers (B2C) and other businesses (B2B).

Examples:

Netflix, Facebook, Amazon.

Key Features

  • Dual marketing strategies: one for consumers, one for businesses.
  • Requires managing different needs of two markets at the same time.
  • Requires managing different needs of two markets at the same time.

5.1.3 - Know the different types of stakeholders

Internal Stakeholders:

Individuals or groups within the organisation who have a direct interest in its success and operations.

Owners

    Individuals or groups that hold legal ownership of the organisation.

  • Interested in profit and business growth.
  • Want a good return on their investment.
  • Often founders or shareholders in smaller firms.

Directors

    Appointed by owners to oversee the organisation's operations and make strategic decisions.

  • Focus on long-term success and sustainability.
  • Responsible for setting company policies and reputaion.
  • Make high-level business decisions.

Employees

    Individuals working for the organisation regulary in various roles.

  • Interested in job security, fair wages, and good working conditions.
  • Want opportunities for career growth and development.
  • Play a key role in day-to-day operations.

External Stakeholders:

Individuals or groups outside the organisation who have an interest in its activities and performance.

Customers/clients

    Individuals or businesses that purchase goods or services from the organisation.

  • Interested in product quality, price, and customer service.
  • Want value for money and a positive customer experience.
  • Can influence business success through their purchasing decisions.

Suppliers

    Businesses or individuals that provide goods or services to the organisation.

  • Interested in maintaining a good business relationship.
  • Want timely payments and clear communication.
  • Can impact production and service delivery through their reliability.

Outsourced Services

    Third-party companies contracted to perform specific tasks or services for the organisation.

  • Interested in clear contracts and fair compensation.
  • Want a good working relationship with the organisation.
  • Can provide specialised skills and cost savings.

Investors/funders

    Individuals or entities that provide capital to the organisation in exchange for equity or debt.

  • Interested in financial returns and business growth.
  • Want transparency and regular updates on business performance.
  • Can influence strategic decisions through their investment.

Government

    Public sector bodies that regulate and oversee business activities.

  • Interested in compliance with laws and regulations.
  • Want businesses to contribute to the economy and society.
  • Can impose taxes, regulations, and incentives that affect business operations.

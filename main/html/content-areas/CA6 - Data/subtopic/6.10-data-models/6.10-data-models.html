6.10.1 Know the types of data models and understand how they organise data into structures

Relational Data Model

    Structure: Data is stored in tables (relations) consisting of rows (records) and columns (fields). Relationships between tables are made using keys (primary and foreign keys).

    Characteristics: Easy to query with SQL, handles complex data efficiently, flexible and widely used.

6.10.2 Know and understand the factors that impact the selection of data models for organising data

    Efficiency of accessing individual items of data: Some models allow faster access. Example: Hierarchical models allow quick traversal if the path is known. Relational models may require searching multiple tables.

    Efficiency of data storage: Some models use less storage. Example: Network models can reduce redundancy compared to hierarchical models.

    Level of complexity in implementation: How difficult it is to build and maintain the database. Example: Hierarchical models are simpler but less flexible. Relational models are more complex but highly versatile.

6.10.3 Understand the benefits and drawbacks of different data models and make judgements about the suitability of data models based on efficiency and complexity

Comparison Table

    Hierarchical:

    • Benefits: Simple to understand, fast access if hierarchy known
    • Drawbacks: Inflexible, difficult to modify, one-to-many only
    • Suitability: Small, predictable datasets

    Network:

    • Benefits: Supports many-to-many relationships, reduces redundancy
    • Drawbacks: Complex to implement, difficult to navigate
    • Suitability: Complex relationships like social networks

    Relational:

    • Benefits: Flexible, supports complex queries, widely used
    • Drawbacks: Can be slower for very large datasets if not optimized
    • Suitability: General-purpose databases, business applications

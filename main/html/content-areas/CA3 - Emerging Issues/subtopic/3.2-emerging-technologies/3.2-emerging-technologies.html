3.2.1 - Understand how developments in technologies impact organisations, individuals and society

1. Storage Media: Increased Demand for Storage

  • Purpose: Store and manage large volumes of data for modern applications, big data, AI, IoT, and multimedia content.
  • Importance: Enables organisations and individuals to access and process large datasets efficiently.
Good Impacts
  • Organisations can analyse large datasets for decision-making and insights.
  • Individuals can access files and cloud services conveniently from anywhere.
  • Society benefits from advancements in healthcare, research, and communication enabled by data storage.
Ethical / Moral Concerns
  • Data privacy risks if personal data is stored or shared without consent.
  • Data security breaches can expose sensitive information.
  • High-cost storage may disadvantage smaller organisations or low-income users.
  • Environmental impact due to energy-intensive data centres.
Impacts on Organisations, Individuals, and Society
  • Organisations: Must implement secure storage, ethical policies, and data compliance practices.
  • Individuals: Convenience but risk of privacy breaches or identity theft.
  • Society: Trust in digital systems may erode; environmental costs from energy consumption.

2. Processing Technologies: Quantum Computing

  • Purpose: Use qubits and quantum mechanics to solve complex problems faster than classical computers.
  • Importance: Accelerates scientific research, AI training, and complex data analysis.
Good Impacts
  • Accelerates drug discovery, climate modelling, and scientific research.
  • Enables faster AI training and complex data analysis.
  • Organisations can optimise logistics, cybersecurity, and operations more efficiently.
Ethical / Moral Concerns
  • Could break current encryption, compromising sensitive data.
  • Access may be limited to wealthy organisations or nations, raising fairness concerns.
  • Dual-use potential for harmful applications like cybercrime or warfare.
Impacts on Organisations, Individuals, and Society
  • Organisations: Must adopt quantum-safe encryption and responsible policies.
  • Individuals: Data privacy may be at risk.
  • Society: Could widen technological inequality and pose security threats.

3. Internet of Things (IoT)

  • Purpose: Connect devices to collect and process data, with edge computing for local processing.
  • Importance: Enhances efficiency, real-time monitoring, and personalisation in multiple contexts.
Good Impacts
  • Improves efficiency in industries, smart cities, and homes.
  • Real-time monitoring enhances safety, energy efficiency, and convenience.
  • Enables personalised services, such as health tracking and smart assistants.
Ethical / Moral Concerns
  • Continuous monitoring can invade privacy and autonomy.
  • Devices are vulnerable to hacking, creating security risks.
  • Users may not fully understand how their data is collected or used.
  • Not all individuals can afford IoT devices, creating inequity.
Impacts on Organisations, Individuals, and Society
  • Organisations: Must ethically manage and secure IoT data.
  • Individuals: Convenience versus risk of privacy invasion.
  • Society: Potential mass surveillance; privacy norms are challenged.

4. Artificial Intelligence (AI)

  • Purpose: Machine learning and generative AI to analyse data, automate tasks, and create content.
  • Importance: Enhances efficiency, personalisation, and innovation across sectors.
Good Impacts
  • Automates repetitive tasks, saving time and costs for organisations.
  • Provides personalised experiences (recommendations, virtual assistants).
  • Speeds up software development and innovation.
  • Enables improved decision-making and predictive analytics.
Ethical / Moral Concerns
  • Bias and discrimination can occur from biased training data.
  • Lack of transparency (“black-box”) makes decisions hard to explain.
  • Job displacement due to automation.
  • Potential misuse for misinformation, deepfakes, or harmful content.
Impacts on Organisations, Individuals, and Society
  • Organisations: Ensure AI fairness, accountability, and transparency.
  • Individuals: Risk of unfair treatment or unemployment.
  • Society: Ethical debates over AI governance, bias, and misinformation.

5. Extended Reality (XR)

  • Purpose: AR overlays digital info onto the real world; VR immerses users in a virtual environment.
  • Importance: Enhances training, education, entertainment, and interactive experiences.
Good Impacts
  • Improves immersive training and education experiences.
  • Enhances entertainment and virtual social interaction.
  • Enables interactive marketing and product demonstrations.
Ethical / Moral Concerns
  • Overuse can cause addiction and mental health issues.
  • VR may distort reality, affecting ethical judgement.
  • AR devices may record surroundings without consent.
  • Excessive use may lead to social isolation.
Impacts on Organisations, Individuals, and Society
  • Organisations: Responsible design and ethical use of XR applications.
  • Individuals: Risks to mental health, social detachment, and privacy.
  • Society: Changes in social norms and potential normalization of harmful virtual content.

6. Open Source Software

  • Purpose: Publicly available code that can be used, modified, and redistributed.
  • Importance: Reduces cost, encourages collaboration, and accelerates innovation.
Good Impacts
  • Reduces software costs for organisations.
  • Provides learning and skill-building opportunities for developers.
  • Encourages collaboration and community-driven improvement.
Ethical / Moral Concerns
  • Security risks if vulnerabilities are not fixed promptly.
  • Intellectual property misuse or failure to credit contributors.
  • Unequal participation or exclusion within projects can occur.
Impacts on Organisations, Individuals, and Society
  • Organisations: Must use responsibly, ensure security, and comply with licensing.
  • Individuals: Developers responsible for ethical contributions and usage.
  • Society: Collaboration benefits innovation, but negligence can harm communities.

7. Blockchain

  • Purpose: Decentralized ledger for secure and transparent transactions.
  • Importance: Enables fraud reduction, secure digital ownership, and financial innovation.
Good Impacts
  • Provides transparency and security in transactions.
  • Reduces fraud and intermediary costs.
  • Enables innovation in finance, supply chains, and digital ownership.
Ethical / Moral Concerns
  • Energy-intensive mining can harm the environment.
  • Can be misused for illegal activities such as money laundering or fraud.
  • Public ledger transparency may compromise privacy.
Impacts on Organisations, Individuals, and Society
  • Organisations: Must balance sustainability, legality, and privacy concerns.
  • Individuals: Secure transactions but potential ethical or privacy risks.
  • Society: Environmental impact and risk of criminal misuse; positive applications exist if regulated.

8. Environmental Impacts of Technology

  • Purpose: Assess the resource use, energy consumption, and disposal impacts of electronic systems.
  • Importance: Supports sustainable technology development and environmental responsibility.
Good Impacts
  • Advances in technology improve quality of life, healthcare, and communication.
  • Organisations can adopt green practices to reduce carbon footprint.
  • Encourages innovation in recycling and sustainable technology design.
Ethical / Moral Concerns
  • Mining for rare metals can harm ecosystems and exploit workers.
  • Improper disposal of electronic waste can pollute the environment and harm health.
  • High energy consumption contributes to climate change.
Impacts on Organisations, Individuals, and Society
  • Organisations: Ethical responsibility to adopt sustainable practices.
  • Individuals: Ethical obligation to recycle and use sustainable devices.
  • Society: Environmental degradation affects communities and ecosystems globally.

9. Autonomous Machines

  • Purpose: Self-driving cars, robotic assembly lines, and drones to automate tasks.
  • Importance: Improves efficiency, safety, and convenience.
Good Impacts
  • Increases organisational efficiency and reduces operational costs.
  • Enhances safety by handling hazardous tasks.
  • Improves convenience and accessibility for individuals.
Ethical / Moral Concerns
  • Decision-making dilemmas in life-or-death scenarios (e.g., autonomous vehicles).
  • Accountability: unclear who is responsible for failures.
  • Job displacement due to automation.
  • Malfunctions may cause serious harm.
Impacts on Organisations, Individuals, and Society
  • Organisations: Liability and ethical deployment are critical.
  • Individuals: Benefit from safety and convenience but risk job loss.
  • Society: Ethical debates on automation, fairness, and regulation.

3.2.2 - Interrelationships Between Digital and Emerging Technologies

Overview

  • Emerging technologies often interact and complement each other to create new capabilities.
  • Understanding these interrelationships allows organisations and developers to make informed decisions, anticipate risks, and leverage opportunities.
  • Impacts can be both positive (innovation, efficiency, accessibility) and negative (privacy issues, bias, environmental concerns).

Key Interrelationships

  • AI + IoT: Smart factories or homes where IoT devices collect data and AI analyses it.
    • Good Impacts: Increased operational efficiency, predictive maintenance, and personalised services.
    • Ethical Concerns: Privacy risks, potential surveillance, data misuse.
    • Impacts: Organisations: efficiency gains; Individuals: convenience vs privacy; Society: improved services but surveillance concerns.
  • AI + XR: VR training systems or AR apps adapting to user performance.
    • Good Impacts: Cost-effective training, immersive learning, engaging experiences.
    • Ethical Concerns: Over-reliance on virtual experiences, mental health risks, privacy issues.
    • Impacts: Organisations: reduced training costs; Individuals: enhanced learning; Society: skill improvement but ethical concerns.
  • Blockchain + IoT: Supply chain tracking with IoT and blockchain verification.
    • Good Impacts: Improved transparency, fraud reduction, trust in products.
    • Ethical Concerns: Energy-intensive processes, potential inequality in access.
    • Impacts: Organisations: traceability; Individuals: safer products; Society: fair trade vs environmental impact.
  • Quantum Computing + AI: Faster training of complex ML models using quantum computing.
    • Good Impacts: Accelerated research, better services, faster innovation.
    • Ethical Concerns: Data security threats, unequal access, dual-use potential.
    • Impacts: Organisations: competitive advantage; Individuals: improved services but privacy risks; Society: scientific breakthroughs vs inequality.
  • Autonomous Machines + AI + IoT: Self-driving cars using sensors (IoT), AI decision-making, and cloud/edge computing.
    • Good Impacts: Increased productivity, safer transport, operational efficiency.
    • Ethical Concerns: Decision-making dilemmas, accountability, job displacement, safety risks.
    • Impacts: Organisations: liability management; Individuals: convenience vs risk; Society: reduced accidents but ethical debates.

Making Judgements on Impacts

  • Organisational Impact: Evaluate efficiency, cost, ethical and legal responsibilities, and strategic advantages.
  • Individual Impact: Consider convenience, personalisation, privacy, job security, and exposure to bias or mental health risks.
  • Societal Impact: Weigh innovation, accessibility, safety, environmental effects, ethical dilemmas, and potential inequalities.

Key Principles for Software Development

  • Holistic Design: Consider combined technology effects rather than isolated features.
  • Ethical Considerations: Protect privacy, security, fairness, and accessibility.
  • Sustainability: Evaluate energy consumption and environmental impact.
  • Risk Management: Anticipate unintended consequences when multiple technologies interact.
  • Inclusivity: Ensure technologies benefit a broad spectrum of users and reduce inequalities.

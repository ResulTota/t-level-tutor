Subtopic 8.1: Security Risks

8.1.1 - Know the type of confidential information held by organisations.

Organisations hold various types of confidential information that are crucial to their operations and reputation.
This information can include:

  • Employees Salaries & Personal Info,
  • Customer & Stakeholders Details,
  • Access Information,   (Passwords, PINs, Biometric Data etc.),
  • Sales Numbers,
  • Marketing Plans,
  • Intellectual Property,
  • Contracts,
  • Financial Records,
  • Trade Secrets,
  • And Business Strategies.

8.1.2 - Understand why information must be kept confidential by organisations.

Information must be kept confidential by organisations to protect the privacy of individuals, maintain competitive advantage, and comply with legal and regulatory requirements.

Confidentiality can:

  • Prevent data breaches,
  • Prevent Identity theft, Fraud, etc.
  • Safeguard sensitive information,
  • Ensure compliance with laws such as GDPR and HIPAA,
  • Protect the organisation's reputation,
  • Maintain customer trust,
  • Avoid financial losses due to fines or legal actions,
  • And ensure the smooth operation of business activities.

8.1.3 - Understand the potential impact to an organisation of failing to maintain privacy and confidentiality.

Failing to maintain privacy and confidentiality can have severe consequences for an organisation, including:

  • Legal Penalties: Non-compliance with data protection laws can result in hefty fines & legal actions.
      e.g: Loss of licence to practise
  • Financial Loss: Compromisation of Data can lead to significant financial losses due to fraud, fines, and loss of business.
  • Reputational Damage: A breach of confidentiality can damage an organisation's reputation, leading to loss of customer trust and loyalty.
  • Operational Disruption: Data breaches can disrupt business operations, leading to downtime and loss of productivity.
  • Competitive Disadvantage: Loss of sensitive information can give competitors an edge, affecting market position.
  • Employee Morale: Breaches can lead to a loss of trust among employees, affecting morale and productivity.
Overall, the failure to maintain privacy and confidentiality can have long-lasting negative effects on an organisation's success and sustainability.
